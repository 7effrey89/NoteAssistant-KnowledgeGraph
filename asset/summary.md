**Summary:**  
The meeting centered on evaluating Power BI capacity solutions, licensing models, and implementation strategies for Aarhus Kommune. Discussions included the feasibility of using F8 capacity for external Power BI solutions, compatibility with internal setups, and addressing the needs of employees without Office packages. Additionally, compliance considerations, governance frameworks, and collaboration opportunities were explored.  

**Key Takeaways:**  
- **Licensing and Capacity:**  
  - F8 capacity can support external users and internal employees without Office packages but requires additional licensing for full functionality.  
  - F64 capacity is the minimum viable option for starting the migration process, with potential to scale up as needed.  
  - Reservation discounts (e.g., 40% for annual commitments) are available, making it cost-effective to start with smaller capacities and scale up gradually.  

- **Implementation Strategy:**  
  - A phased migration approach is recommended, starting with smaller capacities (e.g., F64) and scaling up as more departments migrate.  
  - Transitioning to Power BI will involve overlapping licenses during the migration period, which needs careful budget planning.  
  - Governance and architecture setup are critical for ensuring smooth implementation and cost distribution across departments.  

- **Cost and Budgeting:**  
  - Initial costs for F64 capacity are approximately 44,000 DKK per month, with additional costs for OneLake storage depending on usage.  
  - Pricing varies slightly based on Azure region selection (e.g., Sweden vs. Northern Europe).  
  - Chargeback mechanisms are being developed to allocate costs based on actual usage by departments.  

- **Compliance and Governance:**  
  - Uncertainty regarding compliance rules and whether a large-scale tender is required or if the project can proceed through existing frameworks like SKI needs clarification.  
  - Establishing a robust governance structure is critical to ensure long-term success and alignment across departments.  

- **Collaboration and Partner Recommendations:**  
  - Recommendations for three potential partners with strong compliance expertise will be provided to help identify suitable collaborators.  
  - Aarhus Kommune expressed interest in external support for governance setup, architecture design, and migration planning.  

**Opportunities for Engagement:**  
- Aarhus Kommune is exploring the possibility of using Power BI's chargeback reporting to distribute costs fairly among departments.  
- The municipality is interested in external support for governance setup, architecture design, and migration planning.
